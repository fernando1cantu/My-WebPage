Favorite Movie: Coraline (2009)

Fantasy Horror Stop-Motion 2009

I recommend the movie Coraline because of its unique animation style, dark atmosphere, and meaningful story. The film does a great job of blending fantasy and horror while still delivering an important message about appreciating reality. The visuals, music, and characters create an unsettling yet captivating experience that makes the movie memorable and different from typical animated films.

Coraline follows Coraline Jones, a curious and restless young girl who moves with her parents into the Pink Palace Apartments. Her parents are distant and distracted, focused on writing a gardening catalog, leaving Coraline feeling ignored and bored. While exploring her new home, she meets eccentric neighbors and a mysterious black cat that seems unusually intelligent. Coraline soon discovers a small locked door in her apartment that initially opens to a brick wall—but one night, it transforms into a glowing tunnel that leads her into an alternate version of her world. On the other side, Coraline finds the “Other World,” a place that looks better, brighter, and more exciting than her real life. The Other Mother and Other Father seem loving and attentive, the food is amazing, and everything revolves around Coraline’s happiness. However, there is one disturbing detail: everyone in this world has buttons sewn into their eyes. As Coraline spends more time there, the truth slowly emerges—the Other Mother, also known as the Beldam, is a witch who traps children by luring them with false love and comfort. She plans to sew buttons into Coraline’s eyes, which would trap her soul in the Other World forever, just like the ghost children Coraline later discovers. Realizing the danger, Coraline escapes back to the real world, only to find that the Other Mother has kidnapped her parents. To save them and free the trapped children’s souls, Coraline bravely returns to the Other World and challenges the Other Mother to a game. The rules are simple but dangerous: if Coraline can find the souls of the ghost children and her parents, she wins; if not, she must stay forever. Using her intelligence, courage, and help from the black cat and Wybie, Coraline outsmarts the Other Mother, retrieves the souls, and escapes just in time. Even after returning home, the Other Mother’s severed hand attempts one final attack, but Coraline ultimately destroys it. By the end of the film, Coraline learns to appreciate her imperfect but real life, reconnects with her parents, and grows into a more confident and independent person.
